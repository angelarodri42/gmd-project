Midnight Munchies is finally done and playable on the arcade machine! Yay me! Surprisingly, there were no issues when connecting it to the machine, and everything went smoothly.
Now I can officially say that Midnight Munchies is an endless runner with a small storyline: you play as Angela, who gets a little snacky at midnight but does not have enough chocolate at home. So, naturally, she goes outside to collect more chocolate. If she eats enough, she gets “zoomies” and can attack the green slime balls that, for some reason, are angry at her and make her lose. The player needs to jump over obstacles, collect chocolate, and avoid falling too far behind.
The chocolate bar in the bottom left increases when the player collects chocolate, but it drains over time. It starts at 70%, making it easier to stay above 50%, which allows the player to attack enemies and tree stumps.
The enemies are green slime balls with simple AI behaviour. If the player gets too close, they get angry, change to a reddish hue, and move toward the player. Very rude. Not all obstacles make the player lose, though. Some bushes simply make you fall behind if you dont jump, but they can be used strategically to reach platforms or avoid enemies. Tree stumps, however, are “spiky” and dangerous like the enemies, although they can also be destroyed with chocolate attacks.
The UI is pretty intuitive in my opinion. The game has a main menu, tutorial, pause menu, game over panel, chocolate bar, and score. I do not love that the music restarts when going back to the main menu, so I would fix that if I had more time. However, when I showed the game to friends, they did not seem to notice, so I was probably overthinking that tiny detail. The score is shown in the bottom right, but right now it is only for personal use. In the future, it would be cool to add a leaderboard for the furthest distance travelled.
I also wish I had added the controls directly inside the tutorial. I noticed that I had to explain the controls before people played, but I did not want to risk changing the tutorial right before arcade testing and accidentally breaking something. The final controls are like this:
| Action | PC | Arcade | Arcade Player |
|---|---|---|---|
| Navigate through menu buttons | Arrow up/down/left/right | Joystick | Joystick |
| Select a button | Enter | Button 0 | Green Button |
| Open pause menu | Escape | Button 3 | Yellow Button |
| Close pause menu | Escape | Button 3 | Yellow Button |
| Jump | Space | Button 0 | Green Button |
| Attack | F | Button 1 | Red Button |
| Close tutorial | Arrow up + Enter / Escape | Button 3 | Yellow Button |

When playing on the arcade machine, I also found myself panicking and smacking all three buttons at once when an enemy got close. That can bug the game a bit, so maybe do not do that. If I had more time, I would look into fixing it, because I am definitely the kind of person who panics in games.
Overall, I am super happy with Midnight Munchies. It fits almost exactly how I imagined it, and after the deadline I might look into making it speed up over time or adding multiple levels. I genuinely feel proud of it. It is kind of like my baby!
