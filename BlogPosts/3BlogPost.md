The first milestone focused on the core needs of an endless runner: a character that runs & jumps, ground, and obstacles.
I started with the character sprite. I first searched the Unity Asset Store, but after browsing for way too long, I didnt find anything that matched the image I had in my head. So, naturally, I made my life harder and created the character myself in Piskel (32x32 px). I made an idle sprite, and then worked on running and jumping sprites. The animations were harder than expected because I did not want to use too many frames.
So, I tried using pixie.haus to generate animations from my idle sprite. It sort of worked. The results looked nice, but it generated 52 frames for each running and jumping, which was way more than I needed. In the end, I cherry-picked 2 sprites for running and 1 for jumping. This was also when I remembered Jakob saying we should not spend too much time on sprites. He was right. Unfortunately, having a clear vision is dangerous.
Then, I moved on to the environment. I found assets by BigManJD on the Asset Store, and they matched the theme almost perfectly, except for the daylight background. Luckily, I could adjust the color to nighttime in Unity. Compared to the character sprite situation, this part was much less dramatic.
In Unity, the ground assets were made as a tilemap. I placed around 20 tiles and then decided to keep the character in place while the world moves left. This avoided unnecessary camera movement and worked well for an endless runner. I created a GroundMovement script that moves the ground at a fixed speed, and a GroundSpawner that repositions old ground pieces once they leave the screen. This creates the illusion of infinite ground, kind of like a treadmill.
Once the ground movement worked, I added the running animation and implemented jumping. At this stage, I focused on PC controls first, using the space bar and allowing a maximum of 2 jumps. I used 2D raycast to check if the player was grounded,and reset the jump counter when they landed.
The last main part of this milestone was obstacles. I used obstacle sprites from the same asset pack and created an Obstacles script so they move at the same speed as the ground and are destroyed after leaving the screen. I also made an ObstacleSpawner that randomly chooses an obstacle, so the game does not feel exactly the same every time. Later, I used a similar system for platforms, so pickups could appear above the ground too.
There is still a small issue where some obstacles sometimes spawn on top of each other. It is not game-breaking, but it is something I need to fix in a later milestone. Hopefully, future me has good news about that :)
The final touch came from my boyfriend’s suggestion: making the mountains move too, but slower than the ground. This creates a stronger illusion of movement and means I am technically using parallax scrolling. Look at me, so fancy.
